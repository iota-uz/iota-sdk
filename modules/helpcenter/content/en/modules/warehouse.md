# Warehouse

Warehouse helps teams organize products, units, inventory positions, and orders. It supports the practical work of knowing what exists, where it is, and what needs to move.

## Who uses it

- Warehouse operators
- Procurement teams
- Sales operations
- Inventory managers
- Finance teams that need stock visibility

## What it manages

- Products
- Units of measure
- Inventory records
- Positions and stock rows
- Orders
- Imports for inventory positions

## Why it matters

Inventory mistakes affect sales, purchasing, delivery, and finance. Warehouse keeps stock information structured so teams can make decisions based on current product and quantity data.

## Common tasks

- Create or update a product.
- Define a unit of measure.
- Import stock positions.
- Check current inventory.
- Create or review an order.
- Investigate a mismatch in expected stock.

## Best practices

- Keep product names and units consistent.
- Use imports carefully and review results after upload.
- Check stock data before confirming large orders.
- Coordinate inventory corrections with finance when stock value is affected.
