# CRM

CRM helps teams manage customer relationships from first contact through ongoing support. It keeps client information, conversations, and reusable message templates in one place.

## Who uses it

- Sales teams
- Account managers
- Customer support teams
- Marketing and communication teams

## What it manages

- Clients and customer profiles
- Chats and communication history
- Message templates
- External messaging integrations

## Why it matters

Customer work becomes harder when information is spread across personal inboxes, chat apps, and spreadsheets. CRM gives the team a shared memory of who the customer is, what they asked for, and what happened next.

## Common tasks

- Add or update a client profile.
- Search for a customer conversation.
- Reply using a consistent message template.
- Review recent customer activity before a call.
- Keep contact details accurate.

## Best practices

- Create one client record per real customer or company.
- Keep conversation context inside the CRM whenever possible.
- Use templates for repeated answers, but personalize important messages.
- Review inactive or duplicate client records regularly.
