# Core Administration

Core Administration is where the organization controls access, identity, settings, uploads, dashboards, and shared reference data. It is the foundation for every other module.

## Who uses it

- System administrators
- Operations managers
- Department leads
- Security and compliance owners

## What it manages

- User accounts
- Roles and permissions
- Departments and positions
- Organization settings
- Sessions and sign-in security
- File uploads and shared assets
- Dashboard access

## Why it matters

The ERP should reflect how the company actually works. Core Administration lets a team decide who can view, create, approve, change, or delete information across the system.

Good administration reduces mistakes, protects sensitive records, and keeps every module aligned with the company structure.

## Common tasks

- Invite a new user and assign a role.
- Update access when someone changes teams.
- Review active sessions for security.
- Configure organization details.
- Manage departments and job positions.
- Control who can access reports or settings.

## Best practices

- Use roles for job responsibilities instead of giving broad access to individuals.
- Review permissions after every team change.
- Keep department and position records current.
- Limit administrative access to trusted users.
