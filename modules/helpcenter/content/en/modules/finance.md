# Finance

Finance helps teams record money movement, understand balances, track debts, and review financial performance. It connects operational activity with accounting visibility.

## Who uses it

- Accountants
- Finance managers
- Business owners
- Operations teams that record payments or expenses

## What it manages

- Money accounts
- Payments and payment categories
- Expenses and expense categories
- Counterparties
- Debts and debt aggregates
- Transactions
- Inventory-related financial values
- Cash flow and financial reports

## Why it matters

Finance gives the business a reliable view of where money came from, where it went, and what is still owed. It helps leaders see cash position, spending patterns, customer or supplier balances, and month-end performance.

## Common tasks

- Record a received payment.
- Register an expense and assign a category.
- Track a debt owed by or to a counterparty.
- Review account balances.
- Check cash flow for a period.
- Run financial reports for management review.

## Best practices

- Categorize payments and expenses consistently.
- Keep counterparties clean and avoid duplicates.
- Reconcile money accounts on a regular schedule.
- Review debts before closing a reporting period.
