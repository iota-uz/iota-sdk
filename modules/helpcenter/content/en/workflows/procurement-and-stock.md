# Procurement and Stock

Procurement and stock work focuses on having the right products available, in the right quantities, with reliable financial visibility.

## Typical flow

1. A product or unit is created in Warehouse.
2. Stock positions are imported or updated.
3. Orders are reviewed against available inventory.
4. Supplier or counterparty information is tracked in Finance.
5. Expenses, payments, or debts are recorded.
6. Managers review stock and cash impact together.

## What each module contributes

- Warehouse manages product, unit, inventory, and order data.
- Finance tracks supplier payments, expenses, debts, and account balances.
- Core Administration controls who can adjust inventory or financial records.

## Business checks

- Are product names and units consistent?
- Does the order match available stock?
- Was the inventory import reviewed after upload?
- Is the supplier payment recorded correctly?
- Does the financial category reflect the purchase purpose?

## Best practices

- Avoid creating duplicate products for the same item.
- Review stock-impacting imports before relying on the numbers.
- Coordinate inventory corrections with finance.
- Keep supplier records clean for reporting.
