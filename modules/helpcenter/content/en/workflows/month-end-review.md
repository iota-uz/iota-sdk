# Month-End Review

Month-end review is the routine of checking financial, operational, and management information before closing a reporting period.

## Typical flow

1. Finance reviews payments, expenses, debts, and account balances.
2. Warehouse checks inventory records and stock-impacting changes.
3. CRM and Projects provide context for active customer work.
4. Managers review reports and cash flow.
5. Open issues are corrected before the period is finalized.
6. BI Chat can help summarize trends and questions for review.

## What each module contributes

- Finance provides the primary money records and reports.
- Warehouse gives stock visibility that may affect financial understanding.
- CRM explains customer activity behind revenue or receivables.
- Projects shows work that may affect delivery or billing timing.
- BI Chat helps explore questions and summarize findings.

## Business checks

- Are payment and expense categories consistent?
- Are debts up to date?
- Are stock records reliable?
- Are large customer or supplier changes understood?
- Do reports match the management story for the period?

## Best practices

- Run the same review checklist every period.
- Resolve duplicates before reporting.
- Document unusual changes while the context is fresh.
- Review both cash movement and operational causes.
